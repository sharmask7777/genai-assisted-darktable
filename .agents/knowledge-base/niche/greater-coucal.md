# Greater Coucal (Centropus sinensis)

## Aesthetic: Ruby & Copper
The Crow Pheasant is defined by its high-contrast look: glossy black body, rich copper/chestnut wings, and a piercing ruby-red eye.

### Key Colors to Protect & Enhance:
- **Ruby-Red Eye:** The "soul" of the photo. Ensure the white balance doesn't shift the reds towards orange. Use a surgical drawn mask with 'diffuse or sharpen' to enhance the iris texture.
- **Copper/Chestnut Wings:** These can easily look muddy in shadows. Use AgX with a slight saturation boost (1.1) to keep the warm, metallic glow.
- **Glossy Black Body:** Black feathers reflect blue/green sky light. Ensure the 'tint' is balanced to prevent a green cast in the black plumage.

## Technical Strategy
- **Noise Management:** As Coucals often hide in thickets (low light), noise is common. Use 'denoiseprofile' (wavelets) first. Use 'diffuse or sharpen' (denoise mode) on a second instance restricted to the background bokeh.
- **Exposure (Scene-Referred):** Despite the bird being black, AgX requires a baseline lift (+0.5 to +1.0 EV) to ensure the copper textures are visible and don't "crush" into black.
- **Sharpening:** Apply 'lens deblur' preset in 'diffuse or sharpen' to the wing feathers and head.

## Professional Advice
"A Greater Coucal without a catchlight in its ruby eye is just a silhouette. Lift the exposure until the eye speaks, then use AgX to hold the blacks deep." - Wildlife Mentorship Insight.
