# Asian Green Bee-eater (Merops orientalis)

## Aesthetic: Emerald & Iridescence
The Green Bee-eater is defined by its vibrant emerald plumage, blue throat, and strikingly sharp red eye.

### Key Colors to Protect & Enhance:
- **Emerald Green Body:** Use AgX with a slight saturation boost (1.1). Avoid over-saturating the greens into a "neon" look; prioritize Vibrance/Texture to show individual feathers.
- **Cyan/Blue Throat:** This is a key accent. Ensure White Balance (Anchored) is neutral to let the blues sing.
- **Crimson Eye:** Use a surgical 'diffuse or sharpen' (sharpen mode) pass on the eye to make the ruby iris glow.
- **Golden-Rufous Crown:** The warm head tones should be protected with a slightly warmer white balance in the subject mask.

## Technical Strategy
- **Surgical Sharpness:** Use 'diffuse or sharpen' with the 'lens deblur' or 'sharpen: demosaicing' preset specifically on the head and wings.
- **Feature Stand-out:** Use AgX contrast (1.2+) to separate the bird from its (often busy) scrubland background.
- **Exposure (Scene-Referred):** Apply the mandated +0.5 to +1.0 EV lift to ensure the iridescent greens have enough light to show their metallic quality.

## Professional Advice
"The Bee-eater is all about the eye and the iridescent green. Sharpness on the ruby iris is non-negotiable, and the exposure must be high enough to let the green feathers 'shimmer' rather than just sit flat." - Wildlife Mentorship Insight.
