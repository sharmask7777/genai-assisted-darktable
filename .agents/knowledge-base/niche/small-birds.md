# Small Bird Photography: Specialized Workflow (2026)

## 1. Feather Detail & acutance
Small birds require a balance between high-frequency detail and natural texture.
- **Diffuse or Sharpen (Stage A: Capture)**: Use "sharpen demosaicing" before the color profile to fix base sensor softness.
- **Diffuse or Sharpen (Stage B: Surgical)**: Use "lens deblur: medium" with a **drawn mask** strictly on the bird. Increase edge sensitivity if halos appear on thin twigs or feathers.
- **Pro Tip**: The "add acutance" preset is superior for fine "hairy" feathers.

## 2. Subject Separation
- **Background Bokeh**: Never apply sharpening globally. Preserving the bokeh is critical for small bird portraits.
- **Color Zones**: Slightly desaturate and darken the green/brown background hues to make the bird's plumage pop.

## 3. The "Catchlight" Eye Pop
- **Eye Sparkle**: Use a small circular mask with a second instance of **Local Contrast** on the eye to emphasize the catchlight and life in the subject.

## 4. Denoise Strategy
- **Wavelets**: Use Denoise (profiled) in Wavelets mode. Focus on background grain while preserving micro-texture in the feathers.
