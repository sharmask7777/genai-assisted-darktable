# Bird Photography Cropping Expert Rules

Cropping birds is technically distinct from portraits. The goal is to emphasize the power and elegance of the avian form without making the image feel "cramped."

## 1. Avoid "Joint Cutting"
- **Rule**: NEVER crop through a bird's joint (knees, feet) or the tip of a wing.
- **Action**: If you can't include the whole wing, crop mid-wing (bicep area) with a clear intent, or go for a tight head-and-shoulders portrait.

## 2. Lead Room vs. Tail Room
- **Rule**: Provide 2x more space in front of the beak than behind the tail.
- **Action**: For birds in flight (BIF), provide even more lead room to give the bird "space to fly into."

## 3. Eye Placement
- **Rule**: The eye is the emotional anchor.
- **Action**: In tight crops, place the eye on the upper-left or upper-right intersection of the Rule of Thirds grid.

## 4. Perch Balance
- **Rule**: Don't let the perch (twig) lead the viewer's eye out of the frame.
- **Action**: Level the perch if it's distracting, or use it as a leading line toward the bird's body.
