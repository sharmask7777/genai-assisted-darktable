# Indian Pond Heron (Ardeola grayii)

## Breeding Plumage Aesthetic (May - September)
The "Paddybird" transforms from a camouflaged brown to a vibrant palette.

### Key Colors to Protect & Enhance:
- **Maroon/Wine-Red Back:** Use AgX to maintain the richness of these filamentous feathers without oversaturating them into "plastic" reds.
- **Yellowish-Buff Neck:** This soft tone can easily get lost. Keep a slightly warmer white balance in the subject mask.
- **Pure White Wings:** These are the "flash" of the bird. Negative exposure compensation or high dynamic range mapping (AgX) is essential to keep detail in the whites.

## Technical Strategy
- **Subject Separation:** Use a drawn mask with 'diffuse or sharpen' (sharpen mode) on the eye and the maroon plumes.
- **Background Cleanup:** Use 'diffuse or sharpen' (denoise mode) on the background bokeh to ensure the "messy" wetland habitat doesn't distract.
- **AgX Setting:** Pivot the exposure on the maroon back. Usually, a contrast of 1.25 provides a professional "national geographic" look.

## Professional Advice
"The transition from a drab brown lump to a flash of white wings is the soul of the Pond Heron. Expose for the whites, and let the maroons be rich and deep." - Wildlife Pro Insight.
